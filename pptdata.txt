# PPT Data for EmotionShield Project

## Slide 1: Title Slide
- **Project Name:** EmotionShield – AI-based Emotional Manipulation Detection
- **Subtitle:** Protecting Students from Digital Deception through Explainable AI
- **Theme:** Cybersecurity, Privacy, and AI for Social Good

## Slide 2: The Problem
- **Digital Threats:** Students are often targeted by phishing, scams, and manipulative messages.
- **The Gap:** Traditional filters focus on links/attachments; they miss "Emotional Manipulation."
- **Psychological Exploitation:** Attackers use fear, urgency, and authority to bypass rational thinking.

## Slide 3: The Solution – EmotionShield
- **Project Goal:** A tool that analyzes the *intent* and *emotional tone* of messages.
- **Key Proposition:** Moving from "Is this a virus?" to "Is this trying to manipulate me?"
- **Target Audience:** Students, educational institutions, and non-technical users.

## Slide 4: Technology Stack
- **Backend:** Python & Flask (Robust and scalable web server).
- **Machine Learning:** 
  - **Scikit-learn:** Logistic Regression and TF-IDF Vectorization.
  - **NLTK:** Natural Language Toolkit for lemmatization and stop-word removal.
  - **Pandas/Joblib:** Data handling and model serialization.
- **Frontend:** Vanilla HTML5, CSS3 (Modern Cyber Theme), JavaScript.

## Slide 5: How it Works (The Hybrid Engine)
EmotionShield uses a dual-engine approach:
1. **Engine A: Machine Learning (ML)**
   - Trained on 13,000+ manipulative vs. safe messages.
   - Detects linguistic patterns that human eyes might miss. (Weight: 60%)
2. **Engine B: Rule-Based Logic**
   - Scans for specific triggers: Urgency, Authority, Fear, Reward Bait.
   - Provides human-readable explanations. (Weight: 40%)

## Slide 6: Key Features & Functionality
- **Explainable Risk Score:** 0–100 score with a color-coded risk level (Low/Medium/High).
- **Manipulative Triggers:** Detects "Artificial Urgency" or "Authority Impersonation."
- **Safe Signal Detection:** Recognizes professional and informational tones to reduce false positives.
- **Recommended Action:** Professional advice on how to handle the detected risk.

## Slide 7: Methodology - Data Preprocessing
- **Cleaning:** Lowercasing and removing noise (special characters).
- **Tokenization:** Breaking text into individual words.
- **Lemmatization:** Reducing words to their root form (e.g., "urgent" and "urgency" are treated the same).
- **Vectorization:** Converting text into numerical data using TF-IDF.

## Slide 8: User Interface (UI/UX)
- **Design Philosophy:** Modern, premium "Cybersecurity" aesthetic.
- **Interactive Feedback:** Dynamic risk bars and badge status.
- **Privacy First:** Ephemeral processing—messages are never stored or logged.

## Slide 9: Impact & Future Scope
- **Impact:** Empowers students to recognize manipulation tactics before they become victims.
- **Future Scope:** 
  - Browser extension for real-time email analysis.
  - Support for multiple languages.
  - Integration with campus security reporting systems.

## Slide 10: Conclusion
- EmotionShield is more than a filter; it's an educational tool.
- By providing explanations instead of just "Blocked," it teaches users to stay safe in the digital world.
- **Tagline:** Shielding your emotions, securing your digital life.
